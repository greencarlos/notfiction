It has been frequently remarked that the advice of the sibyl is hedonistic, but it should be noted also that the passage represents an initiatory test, not the moral philosophy of the ancient Babylonians. As in India, centuries later, when a student approaches a teacher to ask the secret of immortal life, he is first put mff with a description of the joys of the mortal (viz., Katha Upanishad, 1: 21, 23-25). Only if he persists is he admitted to the next initiation.
