“The Fmptiness of All Things” (Sanskrit: sunyatä, “voidness”) refers, on the one hand, to the illusory nature of the phenomenal world, and on the other, to the impropriety of attributing such qualities as we may know from our experience of the phenomenal world to the Imperishable.

In the Heavenly Radiance of the Voidness,
There existeth not shadow of thing or concept,
Yet It pervadeth all objects of knowledge;
Obeisance to the Immutable Voidness.
(“Hymn of Milarepa in praise of his teacher,” ibid. p. 137.)
