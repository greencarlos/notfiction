32 The following dream supplies a vivid example of the fusion of opposites in the unconscious: “I dreamed that I had gone into a street of brothels and to one of the girls. As I entered, she changed into a man, who was lying, half clothed, on a sofa. He said: ‘It doesn’t disturb you (that I am now a man)?’ The man looked old, and he had white sideburns. He reminded me of a certain chief forester who was a good friend of my father.” (Wilhelm Stekel, Die Sprache des Traumes, pp. 70-71.) “All dreams,” Dr. Stekel observes, “have a bisexual tendency. Where the bisexuality cannot be perceived, it is hidden in the latent dream content” (ibid., p. 71).
